Getting Started

Go to Belltower.school and click Sign in with Google. Use your school-issued Google account to complete the login. You'll be taken directly to your portal.

If you see an error after signing in, your account may not be set up yet. Contact your school administrator.

Pending account: Your account was created but hasn't been activated yet. Contact your school administrator. They can activate you from the Admin Panel under User Access & Permissions.

Staff records not set up: Your login works but your employee record hasn't been linked yet. Contact an administrator to resolve this from the Admin Panel.

Menu items are shown or hidden based on two things: (1) which modules your school has enabled, and (2) your personal permissions. For example, the Carline link only appears if both the carline module is enabled and you have View Carline permission.

Use Request Access in the Staff Portal to ask your administrator for additional permissions.

The Staff Portal is where all employees submit leave requests, view balances, check licenses, and request access.

The Admin Panel is for administrators to manage staff records, approve leave, run the carline, and more. It's only visible if you've been granted admin access. The "Admin Portal" button in the Staff Portal will appear once you have the right permissions.

Leave Requests (Staff)
  1. Go to Leave in your Staff Portal.
  2. Choose whether substitute / coverage is needed.
  3. Select a Leave Type (e.g., Personal, Sick, Vacation).
  4. Select a Duration (Full Day, Half Day, Partial Day, or Multi Day).
  5. Pick your date(s) — the date picker adjusts automatically based on your duration choice.
  6. Add optional notes, then click Submit Request.

Your request will appear in the history table with a Pending status until reviewed by an administrator.

  • Full Day: one full workday off. Select a single date.
  • Half Day: half of your workday hours. Select a single date.
  • Partial Day: a specific block of hours. Select a date, then choose a start and end time. Hours are rounded up to your school's configured increment.
  • Multi Day: consecutive days across a date range. Select a start and end date. Weekends are automatically excluded.

If you select Yes, the system will flag your request for substitute coverage after it's approved. Your administrator will then assign a substitute or internal coverage person. If your role doesn't typically require coverage (e.g., non-classroom staff), choose No.

Certain leave types like jury duty, bereavement, or professional development days are submitted through the same form but are for record-keeping only and do not reduce your leave balance. A blue notice will appear when you select one of these types.

In your Leave Request History, find the request and click Cancel. You'll be asked to provide a reason.

  • If the request is still Pending, it will be cancelled immediately.
  • If the request was already Approved and the dates are in the future, a cancellation request will be sent to your administrator for review.

A rescind request is for approved leave that has already passed but which you didn't actually use. For example, if you had an approved sick day but came in anyway, you can request that the time be credited back to your balance.

Rescind requests are reviewed by an administrator before any balance adjustment is made.

Rescind requests that have been reviewed and denied will show "Approved (Rescind Denied)." This means the leave stays as used.

Pending requests are reviewed by staff with leave approval permissions. If your request has been pending longer than expected, reach out to your supervisor or school administrator directly. You can also use the Ask a Question button at the bottom of this page.

Leave Management (Admins)

Go to Leave Management from the Admin Panel. Open the Pending Requests tab. Find the request, review the details, and click Approve or Deny. You can optionally add a note visible to the employee.

If the request includes substitute coverage, you'll be prompted to assign coverage after approval.

In Leave Management, go to the Adjust Balances tab. Select the staff member, the leave type, the number of hours to add or subtract, and a reason. All adjustments are logged in the ledger.

In Leave Management → Adjust Balances, you can apply an allotment to a single employee or bulk-apply to all staff at once. Use the Annual Allotments section to run this at the start of each school year.

The Rollover tab in Leave Management shows each staff member's remaining balance. You can review who has unused hours, configure carry-over rules, and process payouts for unused leave. Negative balances (where more leave was taken than earned) are also flagged for review before rollover.

Go to Leave Management → Reports. Choose a report type (Summary, Individual, or Ledger), set your date range and campus filter if needed, then click Export. Reports download as Excel files.

Carline & Dismissal

The Carline operator opens the Carline screen and uses a keypad to enter a family's or carpool's tag number. Pressing Call moves the associated students to a "Called" status, signaling staff inside the school to send those students out.

The system supports Recall (if a student didn't make it out in time and needs to be called again), Undo (to reverse the last action), and All Call (to call all remaining students at once at the end of dismissal).

A carpool tag is a shared pickup tag number assigned to a group of families who ride together. For example, Family A (#100) and Family B (#200) might share Carpool tag #300. When the operator enters #300, students from both Family A and Family B are called out together.

Individual family tags still work as normal. The carpool tag is an additional number, not a replacement.

In the Admin Panel, go to Carpools (under Directory, below Bus Groups). Click Add Carpool to create a new carpool tag number. You can enter a new number or inherit an existing family's tag. Once created, open the carpool to add families to it.

You need Manage Carpools permission to access this section.

Bus groups appear as separate buttons on the carline screen. Clicking a bus group button calls all students assigned to that bus at once. Each bus group button shows how many students have been called vs. how many are waiting, so operators can track progress independently from the car line.

When opening the Carline screen, you'll be prompted to select an active campus (or it will auto-select if there's only one). All student lookups are scoped to that campus, so entering a tag number only calls students enrolled there, even if the same family has children at multiple campuses.

Admin Panel

In the Admin Panel, go to Students or Families in the Directory section. Use the Add button (or the drawer that opens on the right) to enter the new record. Students are linked to families, so create the family first if it doesn't exist.

For adding many records at once, use Bulk Upload with the provided Excel template.

  1. Go to Admin Panel → Bulk Upload.
  2. Download the provided Excel template for the data type you want to import.
  3. Fill in your data following the column headers exactly.
  4. Drag and drop (or browse to) your completed file.
  5. Select the appropriate sheet if prompted, then click Preview to do a dry run.
  6. Review the preview for any errors, then click Commit to save.

If something goes wrong after committing, use Undo Last Upload to roll back the entire batch.

Go to Admin Panel → Exports. Choose a report type: class placements, rosters (by teacher or grade), bus assignments, or contact lists (all families, by grade, or by homeroom). Everything downloads as an Excel file.

Every directory table (Staff, Students, Families, etc.) has a search bar that filters by name and key fields. Use the sort dropdown to change the sort order. Results are paginated and you can use the arrows at the bottom to move between pages.

Access & Permissions

In the Staff Portal, go to Request Access. Check the boxes for the permissions you need, add a brief explanation of why you need them, and click Submit Request. An administrator will review and respond.

You can view the status of your submitted requests in the "My Requests" section below the form and withdraw any pending request you no longer need.

In the Admin Panel, go to User Access & Permissions. Select a staff member from the dropdown. Their current permissions will load as checkboxes you can toggle on or off. Changes save instantly.

Use the Role Presets dropdown to quickly apply a predefined set of permissions (Teacher, Office Staff, or Admin).

When a new staff member signs in for the first time, their account is created with a Pending status. In the Admin Panel under User Access & Permissions, scroll to the Pending Users table at the bottom. Click Activate next to their name.

Activating links their login to their employee record (or creates one if it doesn't exist) and sets their account to active.

  • Active: the user can log in and use Belltower normally.
  • Pending: account created but not yet reviewed by an admin.
  • Inactive: the account has been deactivated and the user cannot log in.

The Manage Class Placement permission controls access to the Class Placement tool. Any user with this permission will see a Class Placement link in their Staff Portal nav and Admin Panel nav (if applicable).

This permission is typically granted to grade-level teachers and team leads during the class placement season — the period in late spring when the school is planning student assignments for the following year. Because placement access is tied to a permission toggle rather than a role, you can turn it on for the relevant staff just before planning begins and remove it once placements are committed.

Year-End Promotion is separate from Class Placement and remains an admin-only function in the Admin Panel.
Substitute Management

In Substitute Management, go to the Pending Coverage tab. This lists all approved leave requests that need a sub. Click a row to open the assignment form, select the substitute (or choose an internal employee for coverage), set the time range, and click Save.

You can also click a pending event on the calendar to open the same assignment form.

In Substitute Management, go to the Directory tab. Click Add Substitute and fill in their name, email, phone, and any notes. Set their status to Active to make them available for assignment.

The assignment appears in the Cancellations tab in Substitute Management. Review the cancellation and mark the assignment as cancelled to remove it from the calendar and free up the substitute.

Licensure & Certifications

In the Admin Panel, go to Licensure. Click Add License and fill in the staff member, license type, license area, grade band, license number, and expiration date. To edit an existing license, click its row in the All Licenses table.

  • Active: current and valid.
  • Expiring: still active but the expiration date is within 90 days.
  • Expired: the expiration date has passed.
  • Pending Renewal: renewal has been initiated but not yet finalized.
  • Suspended: the license has been temporarily suspended.
  • Revoked: the license is permanently revoked.

The Licensure Compliance dashboard automatically highlights licenses expiring within 90 days, grouped into 30/60/90-day buckets. Staff members can see their own expiring licenses on their Staff Portal dashboard.

Individual license alerts can be muted (per license) so that known non-urgent items don't clutter the compliance view.

A provisional license is a temporary authorization granted while a staff member completes the requirements for a full license. It is flagged separately in the compliance view and on individual records so administrators can track which staff are operating under provisional credentials.

Class Placement

Class Placement is a visual drag-and-drop board for assigning students to teachers for the upcoming school year. You create a placement session for a specific grade (e.g., current 2nd graders who will be moving into 3rd grade), drag students across teacher columns to build balanced classes, then Commit to write the homeroom assignments back to student records.

Placement sessions are drafts until committed, so you can adjust freely without affecting live data. The recommended workflow is:

  1. Flag any students for retention in the Students directory (see How do retained students work in placement? below).
  2. Complete and commit all class placement sessions.
  3. Run Year-End Promotion — students advance to the next grade already assigned to their new homeroom.
  1. Open Class Placement — it appears in the Staff Portal nav and Admin Panel nav for any user with the Manage Class Placement permission.
  2. Click New Session.
  3. Select the academic year and the incoming grade — this is the grade students are currently in, not the grade they're moving to. For example, select "2nd Grade" to plan the classes for students who will be entering 3rd grade next year.
  4. Optionally enter a session label and a target class size. The target is used to flag classes that are significantly over or under-sized in the commit warnings.
  5. Search and check the boxes for the teachers who will be receiving students next year.
  6. Click Create Session. All active students in that grade are automatically loaded onto the board as Unplaced.

There are two ways:

  • Drag and drop: Drag a student card from one column and drop it onto another teacher's column.
  • Click to move: Click a student card to select it (it highlights blue). Then click any column header to move the selected student there. Hold Ctrl/Cmd or Shift while clicking cards to select multiple students and move them all at once.

Use Ctrl+Z (or the Undo button in the toolbar) to reverse your last move.

The Auto-Place button distributes all unplaced students evenly across teacher columns as a starting point. You can rearrange freely after.

Students who are repeating a grade appear on the placement board with a Retained badge on their card, so you can identify them at a glance while planning.

Before building your placement sessions, flag any students who will be repeating their current grade:

  1. Go to the Students tab in the Admin Panel.
  2. Open the student's record and check Flag for retention (will repeat current grade).
  3. Save the record. The student will appear in the Students list with a Retention Flagged label.

Once flagged, these students will show up on the placement board that matches their current grade — but that creates a board conflict you need to handle manually. See A retained student is showing up on the wrong board — what do I do? below.

Flag students for retention before building placement sessions so the Retained badge appears on their cards from the start.

When Year-End Promotion runs, students flagged for retention automatically default to the Retain action — you won't have to find and switch them manually in the promotion preview.

Each placement board automatically pulls in all students whose current grade matches the board's incoming grade. This means a retained 1st grader will auto-appear on the 1st → 2nd board — but they're not going to 2nd grade, so they don't belong there. They also won't appear on the K → 1st board because their grade is still 1st, not K.

The recommended workflow is:

  1. On the 1st → 2nd board, select the retained student's card and click Remove from Board in the toolbar. This takes them off that board entirely and keeps the column totals accurate.
  2. Open the K → 1st board and click Add Student in the toolbar.
  3. Search for the retained student by name or student number and click their row to add them. They'll appear in the Unplaced column with a blue Added badge so it's clear they were placed here manually.
  4. Drag them to the appropriate 1st grade teacher column just like any other student.
If you forget to remove a retained student from the higher board and leave them unplaced there, committing that board will not affect their teacher assignment — unplaced students are always skipped during commit. However, removing them is still recommended to keep column counts clean and avoid confusion.

Use the Add Student button in the board toolbar. This lets you add any active student from your school to the board regardless of their current grade — the most common use case is placing a retained student on a lower board (see above), but it also works for mid-year transfers or other exceptions.

  1. Open the placement board and click Add Student.
  2. Type the student's name or student number in the search box.
  3. Click the student's row to add them. Students already on the board won't appear in results.
  4. The student appears in the Unplaced column with a blue Added badge to distinguish them from the rising-grade roster.
  5. Drag them to a teacher column to assign them.

Manually added students are protected from the Sync Students routine — because their grade doesn't match the board's incoming grade, Sync would normally flag them as "departed." The system recognizes them as manually added and leaves them alone.

An Open Position column lets you plan class placement even when a teacher slot is unfilled — for example, if a 3rd-grade teacher is leaving and you haven't hired a replacement yet. You can still distribute students across that column and finalize the rest of the board without waiting on the hire.

To add one, click + Column in the board toolbar, select Open Position, and give the column a label (e.g. "Open — 3rd Grade"). The column appears with an amber header and an Assign Teacher prompt.

  • Students can be moved into and out of an Open Position column just like a real teacher column.
  • The Commit Placement button is blocked as long as any students remain in an Open Position column — you must resolve all open positions before committing.
  • When the hire is made, click Assign Teacher on that column and select the employee. All students in the column are immediately transferred to the real teacher and the commit block is lifted.
  • When cloning a session to a new year, Open Position columns are not carried over — only real teacher columns are copied.
You can also add a new real teacher column mid-session via the same + Column button if a teacher joins the team after planning has already started.

Flags are color-coded labels you can attach to individual students as placement planning notes — for example, "Behavior", "ELL", "EC", "IEP", "Best friends — separate," or gender labels like "Boy" / "Girl" to help balance classes. They appear as colored dots on each student card.

  • Click Manage Flags in the toolbar to create, rename, recolor, or archive flag types for your school. Archived flags no longer appear in future sessions.
  • Click the tag icon on any student card to assign or remove flags for that student.
  • A student's first flag color shows as a colored accent bar on the left edge of their card so flagged students stand out at a glance.
  • Use the filter in the toolbar to show only students with a specific flag, making it easy to check distribution across classes.

When you commit, Belltower checks flag distribution and warns you about potential imbalances:

  • Balanced flags (e.g. Boy/Girl): warns if any class is more than 65% one gender.
  • Concentration flags (e.g. ELL, Behavior, EC): warns if 3 or more students with the same flag are assigned to the same class.

Warnings don't block the commit — they're informational so you can review before finalizing.

Committing writes the homeroom assignments from the board to each student's record. Only students you've placed in a teacher column are updated — students left in the Unplaced column are not changed. The session then shows as Committed.

Before committing, a confirmation dialog shows a summary of how many students will be assigned, how many will remain unplaced, and any flag or class-size warnings. You can review and cancel if anything looks off.

If you need to make changes after committing, click Undo Commit on the session. This restores every student's previous homeroom teacher and reverts the session to Draft so you can adjust and re-commit.

Always commit placements before running Year-End Promotion. That way students advance to the next grade already assigned to their new homeroom teacher.

Click the Clone icon on any session card. You'll be prompted to select the new academic year; the same teacher lineup carries over as a new Draft session with that year's students loaded in. Open Position columns are not cloned — only real teacher columns are copied. Old sessions can be archived from the session list to keep things tidy without permanently deleting them.

On an open board, click the Export CSV button in the board toolbar. The download includes each student's name, student number, incoming grade, and assigned teacher, sorted alphabetically by teacher then student last name.

Committed placements are also available from the Admin Panel under Exports as formatted class rosters.

Placement boards work by pulling in all students whose current grade matches the board's incoming grade. New students enrolling for the first time don't have a "previous" grade — so the trick is to upload them one grade below where they're going. This puts them on the right board without any special handling.

Once uploaded, the full workflow is:

  1. Upload new students via Bulk Upload with the grade one below their target. Leave family and homeroom teacher blank — those can be filled in later.
  2. Open the placement board for the relevant grade (e.g. Pre-K → K for incoming Kindergarteners).
  3. Click Sync Students. The new students appear in the "New students to add" list alongside any existing students already in that grade. Confirm to add them.
  4. Place them into teacher columns just like any other student.
  5. Commit the board. Each student's homeroom teacher is written to their record.
  6. After all boards are committed, run Year-End Promotion. Every student advances one grade — including your new incoming students, who move from their upload grade up to their actual target grade. Retained students stay put automatically.
For incoming Kindergarteners, make sure PK is added to your school's grade list before uploading (Admin Panel → Schools → edit your school). PK is already a recognized grade in Belltower — it just needs to be enabled for your school.

If a placement board for a grade already exists and is in progress when you upload new students, they will appear the next time someone clicks Sync Students on that board — they won't be added automatically, giving you control over when they join the board.

Year-End Promotion

Year-End Promotion updates every student's grade level in Belltower at the close of the school year. Each student is processed in one of three ways:

  • Promote: advances the student to the next grade (default for all students not at the terminal grade).
  • Retain: keeps the student in their current grade for another year.
  • Graduate: marks the student as inactive (default for students at the school's terminal grade).

Year-End Promotion is an admin-only function found in the Admin Panel under the Settings section.

The best practice is to flag students for retention before running class placement, so the placement board reflects who is staying behind. Here's the full workflow:

  1. In the Admin Panel, go to the Students tab and open the student's record.
  2. Check Flag for retention (will repeat current grade) and save. The student will show a Retention Flagged label in the Students list.
  3. When you build the placement session for that student's grade, they will appear on the board with a Retained badge. Place them in a teacher column for the coming year — they're staying in the same grade, so they belong in that grade's session.
  4. Commit the placement session.
  5. When you run Year-End Promotion and load the preview, flagged students automatically default to the Retain action. You don't need to find and switch them manually.
  6. Run Promotion. Each retained student stays in their current grade with their newly assigned homeroom teacher.
You can also change any student's action directly in the promotion preview — just switch Promote to Retain for a student even if they were never pre-flagged.

After Year-End Promotion runs, any student who was retained will show a Retained label on their record in the Students directory for that school year. This is a one-year status marker — it serves as a reminder that this student repeated their grade.

The Retained label is automatically cleared the next time Year-End Promotion runs, so you never have to manually remove it. It will not cause a student to be retained again the following year — only explicitly setting a student to Retain in the promotion preview does that.

The pre-promotion Retention Flagged label (the one you set in the student's record) is also cleared as soon as promotion runs, so there's no cleanup needed after the process completes.

  1. Complete and commit all Class Placement sessions first (see the Class Placement section above).
  2. Go to Year-End Promotion in the Admin Panel.
  3. Select the target academic year and, if applicable, a campus.
  4. Click Load Preview. Every active student loads in a table showing their current grade and default action. Students flagged for retention will already show Retain; all others default to Promote (or Graduate at the terminal grade).
  5. Review the list and override any individual students as needed — use the action dropdown to change Promote to Retain, or Retain to Promote.
  6. When the preview looks correct, click Run Promotion to apply all changes.
Promotion cannot be undone from within Belltower. Double-check the preview before clicking Run Promotion, especially for any students you've manually set to Retain or Graduate.

No — the promotion tool saves your work as a draft automatically while you're working. If you leave and come back, a banner will offer to Restore Draft (reloads your previous preview with all overrides intact) or Discard to start fresh.

Drafts are saved per campus and per school, so if you switch campuses a separate draft is kept for each.

Students with no grade level appear in the preview with no default action and are skipped when promotion runs. Go to the Students directory in the Admin Panel, open the student's record, and assign a grade level before running promotion if they should be included.

Compliance

The Compliance module has two main areas:

  • Background Checks: track background check requests, statuses (pending, submitted, cleared), expiration dates, and optional motor vehicle record (MVR) checks for volunteer drivers.
  • Volunteer Agreements: create digital agreement form templates (e.g., volunteer handbook, photo release), send shareable links to parents/guardians, and track who has signed.

It lives at Compliance in the Admin Panel (Operations section).

  1. Go to Compliance → Background Checks.
  2. Click New Request and enter the person's name, email, reason, and any volunteer roles they're being cleared for.
  3. As the check progresses, open the record and update its status: Pending → Submitted → Cleared.
  4. Enter the cleared date and expiration date when the results come back.

The dashboard shows counts by status and flags any records approaching expiration so nothing falls through the cracks.

  • Pending: the request has been created but not yet submitted to the check provider.
  • Submitted: the request has been sent and is awaiting results.
  • Cleared: the person has passed the background check and is approved to volunteer.
  • Failed: the check returned a disqualifying result.
  • Expired: the cleared date has passed the expiration window and a new check is needed.

Under Compliance → Form Templates, you can create reusable agreement forms with your school name, logo, and agreement text. Each template generates a unique shareable link.

When a guardian or volunteer opens the link, they read the agreement, type their full name as a signature, and submit. Their signed record is captured and visible under Agreements, where you can see who has signed, download a PDF copy, or void an agreement if needed.

Agreements auto-link to a guardian's record when the email on file matches the signer's email, so compliance status updates automatically.

An MVR (Motor Vehicle Record) check is an additional clearance for volunteers who will be driving students — for example, on field trips. Background check records include optional MVR cleared and expiration date fields separate from the standard background check dates.

If your school has the Require MVR for Drivers setting enabled, volunteers flagged as drivers who don't have a valid MVR on file will appear highlighted in the compliance dashboard.

Driver's license and insurance expiration dates are stored on the guardian's record, not the background check itself. To enter them:

  1. Go to Compliance → Background Checks and open the person's background check record.
  2. Scroll to the bottom of the drawer — a Guardian section will load automatically, showing the linked guardian record.
  3. Enter the DL expires and Insurance expires dates, then click Save Changes.
The Guardian section matches the record by email first, then by first and last name as a fallback. If it doesn't appear, make sure the background check record and the guardian record share a consistent email address or spelling.

The Compliance Report (in the staff portal under Compliance Report) shows guardian form-signing status by homeroom. Access works in three tiers:

  • Compliance managers (users with the Manage compliance permission) can see all homerooms school-wide.
  • Homeroom teachers can see their own homeroom automatically — no extra setup needed.
  • Other staff (e.g. a grade-level TA managing field trips) can be granted access to specific teachers' homerooms by a compliance manager.

To grant a staff member report access:

  1. Go to Compliance → Settings.
  2. In the Compliance Report Access section, click New Grant.
  3. Search for the staff member by name or email and click Select.
  4. Choose which teacher's homeroom they should be able to see, then click Save Grant.

You can add multiple grants for the same person (one per teacher). Grants can be revoked at any time from the same Settings page.

Field Trips

Field trips can be created from two places:

  • Staff portal: Go to Field Trips in the left navigation and click New Trip. This is the primary tool for teachers.
  • Admin panel: Go to Field Trips under the Operations section.

In either case, fill in the trip name, destination, dates, departure/return times, and the grade levels attending. You can also set a maximum chaperone count and flag that drivers are needed.

When you save a new trip, you are automatically added as a managing teacher. You can add other teachers during creation or at any time afterward.

Each trip has a list of managing teachers — the staff members responsible for that trip. Only people on this list (plus school admins) can view and edit the trip.

To add a managing teacher: open the trip and use the Add teacher/staff search field in the Managing Teachers section. Type a name or email and select from the results. Any managing teacher on the trip can add or remove others.

To remove a managing teacher: click the × on their chip in the Managing Teachers row.

The Manage field trips permission in the admin access panel is different — it grants a school administrator visibility into all trips school-wide, not just ones they're listed on.

When you open a trip, a Chaperone Form Links panel appears above the tabs (if your school has active volunteer forms marked as required for chaperones). Each form shows a Copy Link button.

Copy and paste the link into an email or message to parents. When a parent clicks the link, they can complete and sign the form online — their response is automatically recorded in the Compliance module and cross-checked against their chaperone record.

If no form links appear, contact an admin — they may need to activate a form link in the Compliance module.

Open a trip and go to the Chaperones tab. Click Add Chaperone and search for a guardian by name or email. Mark the chaperone as a driver if they will be transporting students.

Each chaperone row shows their compliance status automatically pulled from the Compliance module:

  • Cleared — valid background check on file, all required forms signed.
  • Action needed — background check is pending/submitted, MVR needed (if a driver), or forms are missing.
  • Blocked — no background check record or check has expired.
The compliance summary strip at the top of the Chaperones tab gives you a quick count of cleared, action-needed, and blocked chaperones.

The chaperone list links background check records to guardians by email address first, then falls back to matching by first and last name. "No record" means neither match succeeded — the most common causes are:

  • The background check record was created without an email address entered.
  • The email on the guardian record doesn't match the email on the background check record.
  • There's a spelling difference between the name on the guardian record and the name on the background check record.

Hover over the No record chip to see a reminder of what to check. To fix it, make sure the guardian's email in the Admin Panel matches the email on their background check record in Compliance. If no email is on file for either, ensure the name is spelled identically in both places.

Compliance status on a trip is checked against the trip date, not today's date. A background check or MVR that is valid right now will still show as expired or needed if its expiration date falls before the trip date.

For example, if a chaperone's background check expires in September 2026 but the trip is in April 2027, the system correctly flags it — their check will need to be renewed before the trip.

Hover over the status chip to see the specific reason (e.g. "BG check expired by trip date" or "MVR expired by trip date"). Once a renewed check is entered in the Compliance module with an expiration date that covers the trip, the status will update automatically.

Open the trip and go to the Students tab. All active students in the grade levels assigned to the trip are listed automatically. Toggle the checkbox next to any student to mark them as attending or not attending.

The summary row at the bottom shows how many students are confirmed attending out of the total eligible.

  • Upcoming: the trip date is in the future and the trip has not been cancelled.
  • Past: the trip date has passed.
  • Cancelled: the trip was manually marked as cancelled. Cancelled trips are hidden from the default list but visible using the status filter.

The Field Trips module must be enabled for your school. Contact your administrator and ask them to enable the Field Trips module in the school's module settings.

Once enabled, the Field Trips link will appear in the left navigation of the staff portal. You will only see trips that you have been added to as a managing teacher.

When creating or editing a trip, toggle on Requires payment. This reveals the following fields:

  • Student cost — the amount owed per attending student.

Guardian Intake

Guardian Intake lets you collect and update contact information from parents and guardians without requiring them to log in. You create a campaign, share its link, and parents fill in their name, email, phone, relationship, and the students they're associated with. Submissions are then reviewed by your staff before any guardian records are created or updated.

It's found in the Admin Panel under Data Collection.

  1. Go to Admin Panel → Data Collection.
  2. Click New Campaign and give it a name (e.g., "2025–26 Back to School Update").
  3. After saving, a shareable link is displayed immediately. Click Copy Link to copy it to your clipboard.
  4. Paste the link into an email or message to parents. They do not need a Belltower account to fill it out.

You can retrieve the link again at any time by clicking Copy Link in the campaign table, as long as the campaign is still Active.

The guardian sees a simple, branded form asking for:

  • First and last name
  • Email address and cell phone number
  • Relationship to the student(s) (e.g., Mother, Father, Grandparent)
  • The name(s) and grade(s) of their student(s)
  • Whether it's OK to text them

No login is required. When they submit, a confirmation message is shown and the response is recorded for your staff to review.

In the campaign table, click Review on any campaign to open the submissions list. Use the filter bar to narrow by review status or match confidence.

Click Review on an individual submission to open its detail drawer. The system automatically compares the submitted information against existing guardian records:

  • High confidence match — a strong email or name match was found. Fields that differ are highlighted and pre-checked for you to apply.
  • Medium confidence match — a likely but imperfect match. Review carefully before applying.
  • No match — no existing guardian record was found; you'll create a new one.

For matched submissions, check or uncheck individual fields to control exactly which values get written to the existing guardian record.

  • Accept & Update: applies the checked fields from the submission to the matched existing guardian record and marks the submission as Accepted.
  • Create New Guardian: creates a brand-new guardian record from the submitted data. You'll be prompted to link the new guardian to an existing family or create a new one. Use this when the person is genuinely new to your records.
  • Discard: marks the submission as discarded without making any changes. Useful for duplicates or test submissions.
If you make a mistake, click Re-open on a reviewed submission to reset it to Pending and try again.

  • Bulk Accept — available only when all selected submissions are High confidence and Pending. Applies the matching fields to each guardian record in one step.
  • Bulk Discard — marks all selected submissions as discarded.
  • Merge — combine two or more submissions from the same person into a single record (useful when the same guardian submitted more than once).

Staff Requests

Staff Requests lets you create custom internal request forms — for facilities work, IT support, supply orders, or anything else your school needs to track. Administrators build the forms and assign managers. Staff submit through their portal. Managers receive notifications and can update each submission's status.

Administrators manage forms and view all submissions in the Admin Panel → Staff Requests tab. Staff submit requests from the Staff Portal → Submit a Request link in the navigation.

Only users with Admin or Front Office roles can create, edit, and deactivate request forms, and view all submissions. Regular staff members can only submit requests and view their own submission history.

Form managers do not need admin access. Any staff member can be added as a manager on a form and will receive notifications when new submissions come in.
  1. Go to Admin Panel → Staff Requests.
  2. Click + New Form.
  3. Enter a Form Name (e.g., "Facilities Request", "IT Support") and an optional description that staff will see when choosing which form to use.
  4. Make sure Active is checked so the form is visible to staff.
  5. Add your fields under Form Fields (see below).
  6. Add one or more managers who should be notified of new submissions.
  7. Click Create Form.
  • Short Text — a single-line input. Use for brief answers like a room number, asset tag, or name.
  • Paragraph — a multi-line text area. Use for descriptions, detailed notes, or anything that needs more than one sentence.
  • Dropdown — a list of predefined options the submitter selects from. Enter the options as a comma-separated list (e.g., Low, Medium, High). Use when you want to constrain the answer to known values.
  • Date — a date picker. Use for "When do you need this by?" or similar time-sensitive fields.
  • Yes / No — a radio button pair. Use for simple binary questions like "Is this an emergency?"
  • File Upload — lets the submitter attach a photo, PDF, or document. Accepted formats: images, PDFs, Word documents. Use when you need a photo of the issue, a receipt, or a supporting document.

Mark a field Required to prevent submission unless that field is filled in.

Open the form by clicking Edit Form on its card. In the Form Fields list:

  • Reorder: drag and drop rows using the handle (⠿) on the left of each field row.
  • Remove: click the × button at the right of the field row. This removes it from the form for future submissions but does not delete historical responses already collected against that field.

Click Save Changes when done.

Renaming a field label is safe. Historical responses stay fully intact — they just display under the new label name going forward. This is intentional, so fixing a typo like "Descriptions" to "Description" will not affect any past submissions.

Changing a field's type is also preserved for history but may make old answers appear oddly formatted. For example, if you change a Short Text field to a Dropdown, old free-text answers will still display as-is.

Deleting a field is the only action that can affect history. Once a field is removed and saved, its responses are no longer labelled in old submissions. Avoid deleting fields on active forms with existing submissions unless you're certain the data is no longer needed.

Managers are staff members who are notified when a new submission arrives for a given form. They can also update the status of submissions and leave notes for the submitter. Any staff member in your school can be a manager — they do not need admin access.

To add a manager:

  1. Open the form via Edit Form.
  2. In the Managers section at the bottom of the drawer, type a staff member's name or email in the search box.
  3. Select them from the dropdown. They appear as a chip.
  4. Click Save Changes.

To remove a manager, click the × on their chip before saving.

A form with no managers assigned will not send any notifications when submissions come in. Always assign at least one manager to active forms.

Open the form via Edit Form. You have two ways to control visibility:

  • Toggle the Active checkbox at the top of the drawer and click Save Changes.
  • Or click the Deactivate Form / Activate Form button at the bottom of the drawer for a one-click toggle.

Inactive forms do not appear in the Staff Portal, so staff cannot submit new requests against them. All existing submissions and their data are preserved.

  1. From the Staff Portal, click Submit a Request in the side navigation.
  2. The available request forms are shown as cards. Click the one that matches the type of request.
  3. The form fields appear below. Fill in all required fields (marked with *).
  4. Click Submit Request.

After submitting, a confirmation message is shown. The assigned managers are notified by email automatically. Staff can submit another request immediately by clicking Submit Another.

On the Submit a Request page, a My Requests table appears below the form selection area once a staff member has at least one submission. It shows the form name, submission date, current status, and any notes left by a manager.

Statuses are:

  • Pending — received, not yet reviewed.
  • In Review — a manager has seen it and is working on it.
  • Resolved — the request has been completed or closed.

  2. Use the All Forms and All Statuses filters to narrow the list.
  3. Click any row to open the submission detail drawer.
  4. The drawer shows who submitted it, when, and all their answers field by field.
  5. Change the Status dropdown to reflect progress — Pending, In Review, or Resolved.
  6. Optionally add a note in the Manager Notes field. This note is visible to the submitter in their My Requests table.
  7. Click Save.

Resources

Resources is a staff quick link (in the staff portal sidebar) to PDF documents your school has uploaded — things like the employee handbook, field trip forms, or emergency procedures. Every staff member with a Belltower account can see it; nothing extra needs to be turned on.

It depends on the file type. PDFs and images open in a View & Print window instead of downloading — since you always view the document directly rather than saving a copy, everyone is always looking at the current version. Click Print in that window to print it.

Word, Excel, and other file types don't have an in-browser viewer, so those download directly when you click Download — there isn't a way to offer the same view-only protection for those formats.

Go to Admin Panel → Resource Documents (requires the Manage resource documents permission — see Access below). You can drag and drop a file onto the upload box, or click to browse for one. From there you can:

  • Upload a file (any type) with a display title (the title is what staff see — it doesn't need to match the file name). Each document is labeled Print-only (PDFs and images) or Downloadable (everything else) so it's clear how staff will access it.
  • Rename a document — changes the title only, the file stays the same.
  • Replace File — upload a new file that overwrites the existing one in place. Use this when a document is updated (e.g. a new handbook revision) so staff always see the latest version at the same title.
  • Delete — permanently removes the document and its file. This cannot be undone.

Admins can grant the Manage resource documents permission to any staff member from the Access panel — it doesn't require full admin access, so you can hand document upkeep to office staff without giving them anything else.
